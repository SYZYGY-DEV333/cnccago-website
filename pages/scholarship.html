Cooper-Miller Scholarship

×

The Cooper-Miller Scholarship is given each year to a promising young musician with a serious desire to learn organ. The scholarship was established in 2008 in memory of two distinguished organists from Raleigh, NC:

Dr. Harry Cooper (1897-1986) was a member of the AGO from 1920 until his death. He was longtime head of the music department at Meredeth College, organist/choirmaster at Christ Episcopal Church and later First Baptist Church in Raleigh for many years, and served as a consultant for many local organ projects.

Catherine Ritchey Miller (1911-1998) was a member of the AGO from 1933 until her death, and was an honorary member of the Central North Carolina Chapter since 1990. Mrs. Miller was longtime organist at Edenton Street UMC in Raleigh and served later at White Memorial Presbyterian Chuch.

Maximum value of the Scholarship is $1,500, in addition to a two-year student membership in the AGO and a subscription to The American Organist magazine.

Application Information

Applicants are accepted annually; the scholarship committee and judges, in consultation with the winner, will determine how prize funds will be used.


Elegibility

  • Age Range 13-18. For the upper age limit, student should be 18 at the time of audition and still be in high school
  • Intermediate keyboard skills
  • Serious approach; desire to learn organ
  • Recommendation of teachers


Audition Procedures

Auditions are held on the third Saturday in February at a Raleigh-area church. Winner will be announced by March 1.
Applicants should prepare:

  • Two verses of a hymn, to be played as though they were being sung in a service of worship
  • A solo piece appropriate for a worship service
  • A third piece of the applicant's choosing.

Students may choose to perform on either organ or piano.
