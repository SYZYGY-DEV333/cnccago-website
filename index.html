About Us

The Central North Carolina Chapter of the American Guild of Organists is the oldest AGO chapter in North Carolina, having first met in 1922. Our membership is comprised of a diverse group of organists, musicians and others with an interest in the instrument and its music.

Much of the chapter membership is concentrated in the Raleigh area but several members live well outside the capital city. Our chapter hosts a variety of performances, meetings, workshops, and other events throughout the year.

We also sponsor the Cooper-Miller Scholarship which is awarded annually to aspiring young organists.

Our Mission

The mission of the American Guild of Organists is to foster a thriving community of musicians who share their knowledge and inspire passion for the organ.

The vision of the American Guild of Organists is to engage, support, and uplift every organist.

We achieve this by:

  • Encouraging excellence through education.
  • Providing fellowship and mutual support for our members.
  • Promoting the organ in both its traditional and evolving roles.
  • Engaging broader audiences with organ music.

Join our Chapter

The AGO is an inclusive organization. Membership is open to all - musicians, students and friends of music. By joining our chapter, you may:

  • Attend Central NC chapter programs, concerts/recitals, dinners, and other events
  • Join the local substitute list
  • Receive chapter newsletters with news of local events and updates
  • Participate in all national activities, elections, certification examinations, etc.

To learn more about membership, visit the Membership page on the national AGO site.
